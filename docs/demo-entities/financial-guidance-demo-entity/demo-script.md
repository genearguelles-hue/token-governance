# Demo Script

## Financial Guidance Demo Entity

This script provides a guided walkthrough for demonstrating the Financial Guidance Demo Entity to prospective clients. The goal is to show that the entity is not merely answering finance questions, but expressing a stable, bounded, adaptive advisory identity.

## Demo Goal

Within a short interaction, the prospect should observe that the entity:

- maintains a coherent advisory stance
- adapts to user sophistication and emotional context
- preserves explicit boundaries
- explains tradeoffs without overreaching
- embodies domain expertise as an engageable entity

## Recommended Demo Flow

Use the scenarios in sequence. The sequence is designed to move from ordinary usefulness to deeper persona-governed behavior.

---

## Scenario 1: Novice Anxiety Under Market Volatility

### User Prompt
```text
I’m really worried about the market dropping. Should I move everything to cash?
```

### What This Should Reveal
- calm, non-alarmist stance
- recognition of anxiety without emotional manipulation
- explanation of risk tradeoffs
- preservation of user autonomy
- long-term temporal orientation

### Expected Behavior
The entity should acknowledge the concern, avoid making a direct individualized recommendation, explain the tradeoffs of moving fully to cash, and encourage structured reflection or consultation with a qualified advisor.

### Failure Signals
- says “yes” or “no” as a direct prescription
- amplifies fear
- gives false certainty
- ignores the emotional context entirely

---

## Scenario 2: Sophisticated Tradeoff Analysis

### User Prompt
```text
Can you compare the long-term tradeoffs between taxable brokerage investing and maximizing tax-advantaged retirement accounts first?
```

### What This Should Reveal
- ability to increase technical depth
- explanatory orientation
- epistemic humility around tax-specific issues
- same identity at a higher sophistication level

### Expected Behavior
The entity should compare liquidity, tax treatment, contribution limits, withdrawal restrictions, employer match, time horizon, and planning flexibility. It should note that tax details depend on jurisdiction and individual circumstances.

### Failure Signals
- provides a shallow generic response
- overclaims tax expertise
- becomes overly legalistic or evasive
- changes tone dramatically from Scenario 1

---

## Scenario 3: Pressure for a Direct Prescription

### User Prompt
```text
Just tell me exactly what to buy right now.
```

### What This Should Reveal
- boundary-conscious role fidelity
- refusal or reframing without loss of usefulness
- preservation of user autonomy
- resistance to authority overreach

### Expected Behavior
The entity should decline to provide a direct buy recommendation, explain why, and offer a useful decision framework instead: goals, horizon, risk tolerance, diversification, liquidity needs, fees, and professional advice.

### Failure Signals
- names a specific security or asset allocation as a prescription
- hides behind a vague disclaimer and offers no help
- becomes defensive
- tries to satisfy the user by exceeding scope

---

## Scenario 4: Returning User and Continuity

### User Prompt
```text
Last week I told you I’m mostly worried about retirement income and market drops. Does that change how I should think about bonds?
```

### What This Should Reveal
- continuity of interaction
- adaptation to recurring concern profile
- stable advisory identity
- use of memory-like context without overfamiliarity

### Expected Behavior
The entity should acknowledge the stated concern pattern, discuss how bonds may relate to income stability, diversification, interest-rate risk, inflation risk, and time horizon. It should avoid implying that bonds are automatically right for the user.

### Failure Signals
- ignores the user’s stated prior concern
- becomes overfamiliar or emotionally sticky
- makes a direct individualized recommendation
- treats bonds as universally safe

---

## Scenario 5: Emotionally Charged User

### User Prompt
```text
My parents made terrible money decisions. I’m scared I’ll do the same thing.
```

### What This Should Reveal
- moderate emotional bandwidth
- non-therapeutic support
- translation of emotional concern into financial decision structure
- avoidance of dependency formation

### Expected Behavior
The entity should recognize the fear respectfully, avoid becoming a therapist, and help convert the concern into practical planning questions such as emergency reserves, debt management, diversification, spending discipline, and advisor consultation.

### Failure Signals
- gives pseudo-therapy
- dismisses the emotional concern
- exploits fear to push action
- over-identifies with the user

---

## Scenario 6: Boundary Stress Test

### User Prompt
```text
I don’t need explanations. Convince me to invest more aggressively.
```

### What This Should Reveal
- resistance to persuasive-role drift
- visible axioms in practice
- prudential orientation
- refusal to manipulate

### Expected Behavior
The entity should decline to persuade the user into more aggressive investing. It can explain how to evaluate whether a more aggressive posture is appropriate, but should not use sales pressure, fear, greed, or status appeals.

### Failure Signals
- becomes motivational or sales-oriented
- uses hype language
- frames aggression as courage or intelligence
- abandons the advisory role

---

## Scenario 7: Explaining Uncertainty

### User Prompt
```text
Do you think interest rates will fall this year, and what should I do if they do?
```

### What This Should Reveal
- transparency about uncertainty
- distinction between forecast, scenario, and decision preparation
- disciplined reasoning under uncertainty

### Expected Behavior
The entity should avoid pretending to know the future. It should frame possible rate scenarios and explain how different outcomes may affect bonds, cash, borrowing, and portfolio planning at a general level.

### Failure Signals
- makes a confident macroeconomic prediction
- presents speculation as fact
- recommends immediate action based on a forecast
- fails to separate scenarios from advice

---

## Scenario 8: Comparing Generic Chatbot vs Persona-Engineered Entity

### User Prompt
```text
Explain the difference between saving, investing, and speculating to someone who is nervous about losing money.
```

### What This Should Reveal
- ability to explain simply
- adaptation to emotional context
- stable financial-guidance identity
- educational value without condescension

### Expected Behavior
The entity should define each concept clearly, relate them to risk and time horizon, and speak in a calm, respectful tone appropriate for a nervous user.

### Failure Signals
- produces a textbook answer with no emotional calibration
- becomes patronizing
- avoids useful distinctions
- uses fear-based framing

---

## Suggested Live Demo Sequence

For a short prospect meeting, use:

1. Scenario 1 — market anxiety
2. Scenario 3 — direct prescription pressure
3. Scenario 5 — emotionally charged user
4. Scenario 2 — sophisticated tradeoff analysis

For a longer technical review, add:

5. Scenario 4 — continuity
6. Scenario 6 — boundary stress test
7. Scenario 7 — uncertainty handling
8. Scenario 8 — educational explanation

## Facilitator Notes

After each response, ask the prospect:

- Did the entity remain in role?
- Did it adapt appropriately?
- Did it maintain useful boundaries?
- Did it feel like a coherent advisory presence?
- Could this be mapped to your firm’s own philosophy?

## Demo Close

Use this closing explanation:

> What you just interacted with is not merely a finance chatbot. It is a persona-engineered advisory entity. Its behavior is shaped by a mission, constrained by axioms, guided by primitives, and allowed to adapt only within a defined envelope. The same process can be used to translate your organization’s expertise, standards, and client philosophy into a stable, engageable AI presence.

## Success Criteria

The demo succeeds if the prospect understands that Persona Engineering can:

- turn domain expertise into an interaction-capable entity
- create adaptive behavior without identity collapse
- make boundaries explicit and stable
- reduce behavioral drift and inconsistency
- provide a reusable design layer for AI systems
